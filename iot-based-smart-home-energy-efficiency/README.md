# IoT-Based Smart Home Energy Efficiency Project

This project explores the use of **Internet of Things (IoT)** technologies to improve **energy efficiency in residential environments**.

The focus is on smart home systems that combine sensors, basic automation logic, and data-driven monitoring to reduce energy consumption and support more sustainable living practices.

Residential buildings play a significant role in overall energy consumption and carbon emissions.  
Smart home solutions enabled by IoT technologies offer an opportunity to monitor usage patterns, automate energy control, and increase user awareness.

This project was developed as an academic challenge and focuses on **conceptual system design and sustainability-oriented reasoning**, rather than hardware implementation.

---

##  Objectives

- Explore IoT-based solutions for smart home energy efficiency  
- Identify key sensors and system components  
- Design a basic smart home architecture for energy monitoring  
- Evaluate sustainability and energy-saving potential  
- Link IoT technologies to carbon reduction strategies  

---

##  System Design & Approach

The project proposes a conceptual smart home system composed of:

- **Sensors**  
  Monitoring environmental and usage parameters (e.g., temperature, occupancy, energy usage)

- **Data collection & communication**  
  Basic IoT communication principles for transmitting sensor data

- **Automation logic**  
  Simple rule-based actions to optimize energy consumption

- **User awareness**  
  Feedback mechanisms to support informed energy-related decisions

The emphasis is on **system-level thinking**, not low-level hardware design.
